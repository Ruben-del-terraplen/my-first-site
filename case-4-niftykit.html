§ 01 — Context

A startup explaining
five products at once.

The problem

NiftyKit had grown from a single SDK into a five-product suite, but the marketing site still spoke as if it were one tool. Visitors couldn't tell what the company actually did — bounce on the homepage was 71%, and qualified-signup rate was sub-1%.

The founders wanted a new site, but the real problem was deeper: the company's information architecture didn't match its product reality. We needed to redesign both — together.

"We don't need a marketing site. We need a product map people can read in fifteen seconds." — Co-founder, kickoff

§ 03 — Process

Designing the company,
then designing the page.

P. 01
Information architecture audit
Mapped every external surface — site, docs, in-app, support — and identified five competing taxonomies. Worked with founders to consolidate into one company-wide vocabulary before touching the visual design.
P. 02
Modular service pattern
Designed a single repeatable "service module" with consistent slots — name, one-liner, visual, key benefit, CTA — that could host all five products without each feeling templated.
P. 03
Continuous onboarding
Eliminated the marketing-to-product seam: the same scroll narrative continued into the in-app first-run, with the user's chosen service highlighted. Treated marketing and onboarding as one experience.
P. 04
Founder partnership
Worked side-by-side with founders on copy and positioning — design and message had to evolve together. Shipped the new site with simultaneous brand-voice updates across docs, social, and email.

§ 05 — Reflection

A founding
design lesson.

Looking back

Working closely with founders taught me that the brief is almost never the real problem. They asked for a marketing site; what we shipped was a re-articulation of the company itself. Strategy lives upstream of pixels.

This was also where I learned to lead design alongside non-designer leaders. Translating "the product feels confusing" into design moves — and back into business language — became one of the most useful skills I carry into every role since.
